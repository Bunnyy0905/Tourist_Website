Srinagar, Jammu & Kashmir

Srinagar is a destination situated in the union territory of Jammu & Kashmir, along the banks of the serene river Jhelum. Renowned for its stationary houseboats and traditional rowboats known as Shikaras on Dal Lake, Srinagar offers a dreamlike experience. The city is adorned with the tranquil Dal Lake and Nigeen Lake, making it an idyllic setting for honeymooners and family vacations.

Best Time: April to October

Manali, Himanchal Pradesh

Nestled at the northern end of the Kullu Valley in Himachal Pradesh, Manali is a magical hill station renowned for its spectacular valleys, breathtaking views, and snow-capped mountains. Gifted by the majestic Himalayas, it has become one of the most popular destinations for honeymooners. The town is surrounded by lush forests of oak, deodar, and pine, enhancing its natural beauty.

Best Time: October to June

Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal, situated on the banks of the River Yamuna in Agra, stands as one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Commissioned by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, it serves as a majestic memorial for his third wife, Mumtaz Mahal, and also houses Shah Jahan's own tomb. It is crafted entirely from white marble, showcasing breathtaking architectural beauty.

Best Time: March to June

Udaipur, Rajasthan

Udaipur, often hailed as the "City of Lakes," is a captivating jewel in the crown of Rajasthan. Set against the backdrop of the Aravalli Range, this city beckons visitors with its regal charm, historical grandeur, and breathtaking landscapes. Surrounded by the Aravalli Hills, Udaipur is renowned for its azure lakes, magnificent palaces, vibrant culture, and delectable cuisine.

Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas at the confluence of the Ganga and Chandrabhaga rivers, Rishikesh is a quaint town in the Dehradun district of Uttarakhand, near Haridwar. Also referred to as Hrishikesh, this destination is celebrated for its adventure activities, ancient temples, popular cafes, and holds the esteemed title of the "Yoga Capital of the World."

Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

Hawa Mahal, meaning the "Palace of Winds," earns its name from a distinctive lattice-like structure composed of small windows designed to usher in cool winds and maintain comfort during hot summers. Constructed with a specific purpose, the palace, built to perfection, enabled the women of the royal house to discreetly observe street festivities while remaining unseen from the outside, in accordance with local customs.

Best Time: October to March
